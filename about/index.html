About me

This page is focused on my career life.

I am lucky to experience or have experienced different roles in my life. The following are the most typical ones.

Started as an 

Educator

When it comes to making a difference in the world, educators have been on top of the list. Two years ago, I realized my dream to be an educator and successfully landed a part-time teaching job at Northern Arizona University (NAU) and later a full-time position at Academy of Math and Science (AMS), a K-8 school in Phoenix, Arizona, United States. 

As an educator, it has always been my passion and determination to enlighten students and help them capture the “aha” moments. They also reminded me of capturing my sparkles and pushing my limits higher. These years, I have gradually solidified my interests and passion in  linguistics & computer science, especially data science and natural language processing (NLP). I have reinforced my aspiration to be a computational linguist and data scientist.

with the stepping stone of being a

Language Enthusiast & Translator

I found myself passionate in languages at a young age, when I quickly picked up dialects that my family members were speaking - Xiang Chinese (also with different variations: Urban Changsha, Ningxiang, and Xiangtan) and Cantonese. In middle school, I started to expose myself to a second foreign language (French) and I started to learn it systematically when I was in college. I am learning Spanish and Finnish at the moment. By obtaining my first master's degree in Translation at the Chinese University of Hong Kong (CUHK), I also honed my skills in translation and interpretation. I am happy to have worked for National Collegiate Athletic Association (NCAA) and PAC-12 with Arizona State University (ASU), and for Institue Français (French Institute) with Embassy of France in China, as well as other organizations when I was working part-time as a translator and interpreter.

My goal is to be a

Linguist & Data Scientist

My never-ending interests in computers date back to my childhood, when my parents opened a new Pentium III computer right from the box. At the age of 6, excited and surprised, I started my journey to fiddle around with my new toy. I started coding in Python 2.x when I was in high school. At Hunan University (HNU) in China, I was working part-time as a Full Stack Developer in 1000 Internet and Culture Studio (link is in Chinese), a student-operated studio. In Spring 2021 at NAU, I was fortunate to take Programming for Text Analysis with Dr. Egbert, and it further thrusted my passion for integrating computer science and linguistics. Presently, as a Foreign Language Teacher in Phoenix, I always strive to make a tight integration of my current job and future envisions. Elected as the Director of the AMS South Mountain Robotics and Programming Club, I am actively playing a leading role in the Club started in January 2022. 

Propelled by ambition and desire to become a well-developed linguist and data scientist, I also keep sharpening my coding and data analysis skills (Python & R) on DataCamp and Udemy. Meanwhile, I have also taught myself algorithms and statistical methods regarding NLP and computational linguistics. I am also glad I have been, once again, an active GitHub stargazer and user since the end of 2021.
